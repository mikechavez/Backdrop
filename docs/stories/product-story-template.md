## The Core Narrative

### Situation
*What was the context? What system, product, or team were you operating in?
What was the state of things before you got involved?*

### Problem
*What was broken, missing, or unknown? Why did it matter?
Frame as before-state — what would have happened if no one fixed this?*

### Approach
*What did you do, and why that approach specifically?
Include decisions made, tradeoffs considered, methods chosen.*

### Finding or Outcome
*What did you actually learn or produce?
Be specific. If numbers exist, use them. If the answer was "no," say so.*

### The Non-Obvious Layer
*What would a less rigorous person have missed or gotten wrong?
This is the sophistication signal.*

### Impact
*What changed as a result? What decision did this enable or prevent?
If impact is future-gated, say so honestly.*

## Constraints (Read These)

**On stubs:**
- Must take 10 minutes or less
- If it feels polished, you did too much — delete details and redo
- Incomplete is fine; messy is good

**On expanding:**
- Only expand if "Publishable = Yes" and you have time
- Do not expand a "Maybe" — let it sit 2–3 sprints first

**On the candidate/ folder:**
- Max 3 items at a time
- Review every 2–3 sprints
- Force a decision: publish or archive (don't let them linger)

**On cross-linking:**
- Even stubs must link to 1 decision if it exists
- Add "none yet" if nothing applies
- This is not optional

**On the non-obvious insight:**
- Capture this first, before you write anything else
- This is what you'll tweet, this is what interviewers remember
- If you can't articulate it in 2 lines, expand the full story first
